# UX/UI Design Proposal 2: The Editorial Portfolio (Journalistic)

## Concept
A design inspired by high-end digital magazines and editorial layouts. It emphasizes the written content, storytelling, and the "living portfolio" aspect of the site.

## Visual Language
- **Typography:** A sophisticated serif font (like Playfair Display or Merriweather) for large headings and quotes (especially the "Never stop learning" motto). A highly readable sans-serif (like system-ui or Roboto) for body text and navigation.
- **Color Palette:** Warm, muted, and earthy tones.
  - Dark Mode: Charcoal background (`#1C1917`) with off-white text (`#FAFAF9`). Accents in soft gold or muted terracotta.
  - Light Mode: Warm off-white/cream background (`#FAFAF9`) with deep charcoal text (`#1C1917`).
- **Borders & Shapes:** Generous whitespace, elegant typography scales, and subtle dividing lines (rules) rather than enclosed cards. Images (if used) have soft edges.

## Layout & Structure
- **Navigation:** A sticky, minimalist top or side navigation that fades slightly when scrolling down to give focus to the content.
- **Home Page Hero:** An editorial-style layout. The motto "Never stop learning" is treated as a prominent pull-quote. The layout feels more like a beautifully typeset article introducing Iskander.
- **Cards (Blog/Tools):** Listed more like an index or table of contents. Focus on the title and a thoughtful excerpt, separated by clean horizontal lines rather than boxed cards.

## Vibe
Reflective, experienced, authoritative but approachable. Appeals to leadership (CTOs, Engineering Managers) by projecting wisdom, communication skills, and a thoughtful approach to software engineering.
