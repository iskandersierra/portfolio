# UX/UI Design Proposal 1: The Modern Minimalist (Terminal Inspired)

## Concept

A clean, high-contrast design that nods to the developer experience without going full "hacker mode". It feels like a modern, polished IDE or terminal.

## Visual Language

- **Typography:** A strong monospaced font (like JetBrains Mono) for headings, navigation, and metadata (dates, tags). A clean sans-serif (Inter) for body copy to ensure readability.
- **Color Palette:**
  - Dark Mode Default: Deep slate background (`#0F172A`) with bright, neon-ish accents (electric teal or bright indigo) for links and active states.
  - Light Mode: Crisp white background (`#FFFFFF`) with dark slate text (`#0F172A`) and the same vivid accents.
- **Borders & Shapes:** Sharp corners, thin 1px borders around cards and sections (`#334155` in dark mode). Very minimal drop shadows; relies on borders for depth.

## Layout & Structure

- **Navigation:** A simple top bar. The active page has a subtle block highlight or an underscore, mimicking a cursor.
- **Home Page Hero:** Text-heavy. Large, bold statement: "Never stop learning." followed by a brief, punchy intro paragraph. A simple "blinking cursor" animation at the end of the intro.
- **Cards (Blog/Tools):** Look like command-line outputs or simple code blocks. Title, subtle metadata, and an arrow `->` instead of a traditional "Read More" button.

## Vibe

Technical, serious, efficient, and precise. Appeals strongly to other developers and technical recruiters who appreciate a no-nonsense, fast, and structured environment.
