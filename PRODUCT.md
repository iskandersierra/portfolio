# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Primary visitors are recruiters, CTOs, engineering managers, consulting clients, peer developers, and people already in Iskander Sierra's network. They arrive to assess capability, judgment, technical depth, leadership range, and professional character quickly. The site has to work for fast scanning first, then reward deeper reading through blog posts, project pages, and the about narrative.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio is a living professional home on the web for Iskander Sierra. It exists to communicate seniority, active practice, and personal point of view through a small set of clear surfaces: home, about, blog, and projects. Success means a visitor can understand who Iskander is, what kind of systems he builds, and why his work is worth following within the first few moments, then find concrete evidence through writing and projects.

## Brand Personality

Warm, precise, experienced.

The tone should feel calm, direct, and authored. It should read like someone with deep experience who is still actively learning, building, and shipping. The emotional target is confidence without performance, curiosity without novelty-chasing, and professionalism without corporate polish.

## Anti-references

Avoid glossy SaaS landing-page patterns, neon-on-black developer-tool theatrics, decorative editorial-magazine tropes used as a shortcut for taste, oversized metric blocks, gradient text, and overly ornamental visual effects that compete with the writing. The site should not feel like a template, a startup homepage, or a retro terminal costume.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with authored signal. Every primary page should communicate judgment and point of view before it communicates detail.
2. Let proof follow philosophy. Claims about experience and learning should be backed quickly by visible work, timeline evidence, and concrete outputs.
3. Keep the interface quiet enough for content to carry weight. Visual decisions should sharpen the message, not decorate it.
4. Balance professional depth with personal presence. The site must show the human behind the engineering without drifting into self-mythology.
5. Design for revisiting. Writing and projects should feel like an evolving practice, not a frozen résumé.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard-first navigation, visible focus states, strong contrast in both themes, reduced-motion preferences, and semantic structures that remain clear for assistive technologies. Do not rely on color alone to communicate state or hierarchy.
